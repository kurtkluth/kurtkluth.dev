Open [skyroute.kluthstudios.com](https://skyroute.kluthstudios.com) and you arrive
at the flight setup screen. Work through it top to bottom, start the flight, and
you are sitting on the runway. Here is the whole loop, from setup to touchdown.

## The flight setup screen

### 1. Aircraft

Two choices: the **SL-9 Skylark**, a slow and forgiving piston trainer, and the
**SwiftJet 100**, a fast regional twin-jet that punishes sloppy descents. Learning?
Take the Skylark. The full case for each is in
[Aircraft and Regions](./aircraft-and-regions.md).

### 2. Region

Four regions, each with its own scenery and its own trap: Verdant Coast, Amber
Mesa, Granite Range, and Azure Atoll.

### 3. Route direction

Each region is an airport pair, and you pick which way to fly it. On the Verdant
Coast, for example, that pair is Meridian Intl (MER) and Skyhaven Regional (SKY).

### 4. Weather

Eight options: **Clear, Scattered, Cloudy, Overcast, Foggy, Rain, Stormy**, and
**Crosswind**. Clear is a sightseeing flight; Foggy hides the runway until late;
Crosswind makes the landing itself the boss fight.

### 5. Time of day

**Day**, **Sunset**, or **Night**. Sunset is the pretty one. Night is the honest one.

### 6. Realism

- **Arcade.** The plane mostly does what you mean.
- **Assisted.** Real-ish handling with guard rails.
- **Realistic.** You get exactly the flight you deserve.
- **Custom.** Mix your own.

### 7. Graphics

**High detail** or **Fast**. If the frame rate ever stutters, Fast keeps the flying
smooth, and the sky still looks like sky.

## Your first flight

### Takeoff

1. Brakes off with `B`.
2. Full throttle with `W` and let the speed build down the runway.
3. At about 65 kt, rotate. Press and hold `↓` to pull the nose up. ("Rotate" is
   pilot speak for raising the nose so the plane flies itself off the runway.)
4. Climb straight ahead, then retract the flaps with `Shift+F` and raise the gear
   with `G`.

### Cruise

Level off, set a comfortable throttle, and follow your route. The minimap (`M`)
keeps the destination honest. This is also the perfect moment to hand the plane to
the [autopilot](./autopilot.md) with `P` and enjoy the view.

### Landing

The scoring cares about one thing above all: touch down gently, "on the numbers"
(the big painted digits at the start of the runway).

- Slow down early, extend flaps with `F`, and drop the gear with `G`.
- Aim for the numbers and keep the descent shallow. Under about 300 fpm (feet per
  minute of descent) at touchdown is the target for a high score.
- After touchdown: brakes with `B`, and in the SwiftJet hold `S` at idle for
  reverse thrust.

:::tip

Flare late and small. If the runway is rushing up at you, add a touch of power. A
long gentle landing beats a short firm one every single time.

:::
