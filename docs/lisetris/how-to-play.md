Lisetris plays the way falling-block games have always played, which
means you already half know it. Here is the other half.

## The goal

Pieces fall into the well one at a time. Move, rotate, and drop each one
to build complete horizontal rows; a completed row clears, and everything
above it settles down. Keep the stack low and the rows clearing.

In **Classic**, speed rises as you clear rows, and the game ends when the
well tops out, when the stack climbs so high that new pieces have no
room to enter. In **Relaxed**, the same well is a gentler place to be.

## Choosing a mode

From the main menu, choose Play and pick your evening:

- **Classic.** Speed rises as you clear rows. Play until the well tops
  out. This is the mode with the pounding heart.
- **Relaxed.** For learning the controls, unwinding, or admiring the neon.

## Controls

| Action | Keys |
|---|---|
| Move left | `←` or `A` |
| Move right | `→` or `D` |
| Soft drop | `↓` or `S` |
| Hard drop | `Space` |
| Rotate | `↑` or `X` |
| Rotate back | `Z` |
| Hold | `C` or `Shift` |
| Pause | `P` or `Esc` |
| Mute | `M` |

### Soft drop vs hard drop

- **Soft drop** (`↓` or `S`) hurries the piece downward while you keep
  full control; you can still slide and rotate all the way to the floor.
  It is the careful player's accelerator.
- **Hard drop** (`Space`) commits. The piece slams straight down to where
  it was headed, instantly. Fast, decisive, and unforgiving of a placement
  you had not quite finished thinking about.

Learn with soft drop; graduate to hard drop when your placements stop
surprising you.

### The hold slot

Press `C` or `Shift` to tuck the current piece away for later. Hold is a
pocket for the piece that deserves a better spot than the stack can offer
right now, or the escape hatch when the falling piece is exactly the
wrong shape for the moment. More on using it well in [Tips](./tips.md).

## Pause and quiet

`P` or `Esc` pauses the game when life interrupts. `M` mutes the sound
when you need the neon without the noise.
