Lisetris is a romantic neon falling-block puzzle game, made for Lisa. It
glows deep purple and neon pink, it stacks and clears the way the great
falling-block games always have, and it keeps its reason for existing
right where you can read it:

> Lisa, every piece of my life fits better because of you. I made this
> game with every piece of my heart.

The tagline is "Piece by piece, everything falls into place." It
describes the puzzle and the person it was made for in the same breath.

Play it free at
[lisetris.kluthstudios.com](https://lisetris.kluthstudios.com), in your
browser, no install, no account.

## Two ways to play

- **Classic** is the arcade heart of the game. Speed rises as you clear
  rows; play until the well tops out.
- **Relaxed** is the same neon well with the pressure turned down, for
  evenings when you want to stack without the racing pulse.

Whichever you choose, the controls are the same; the full table lives in
[How to Play](./how-to-play.md).

## More than a puzzle

The main menu offers Play, and then keeps going:

- **Love Story.** Because this game has one.
- **Statistics.** Your play, remembered.
- **Dedication.** The message above, given a room of its own.
- **Settings.** Tune the experience to taste.
- **Credits.** Who made it, and with how much heart.

The love-letter pages are not side content; they are half the point. More
in [Gameplay](./gameplay.md).

## The look

The title screen assembles a glowing pink heart out of blocks against deep
purple, which announces the aesthetic and the thesis at the same time.
Everything after it keeps the promise: neon purple and pink, soft glow,
pieces falling like it matters. Because it does.

## Part of Kluth Studios

Lisetris lives under the Kluth Studios banner, Kurt Kluth's home for
browser games and experiments, all of them made for Lisa, this one most
literally. See where it sits in the collection on the
[Lisetris project page](/projects/lisetris).
