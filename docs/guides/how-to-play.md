Every Kluth Studios game follows the same philosophy. Click the link, play
immediately. This page covers what they all share, then hands you off to each
game's own how-to-play page for the specifics.

## What the games have in common

- **Instant browser play.** Open the live URL and you're in. No install, no
  account, no payment, ever.
- **Keyboard-first, with touch where it fits.** Every game plays great on a
  keyboard. Lisa Climber, Lisetris, and Spindrift also support touch, and
  Lisa's Tapistry is built for touch first, so phones and tablets work
  well. Skyroute has enough controls that it strongly prefers a keyboard.
- **Scores and progress live in your browser.** High scores, stats, and
  settings are stored locally on the device you play on; nothing is sent
  to a server.
- **Near-universal keys.** `P` or `Esc` pauses and `M` mutes in almost
  every game. Skyroute is the one that reshuffles the deck. There, `Esc`
  pauses, `V` toggles sound, `M` opens the minimap, and `P` engages the
  autopilot.

:::note

Because everything is stored locally, a different browser, a different
device, or a private window starts fresh; there are no accounts and no
cloud sync. Guard the browser that holds your high scores.

:::

## Jump in, game by game

### Lisa Climber

Summit Smash is a vertical climb. Six stages, eight layers each, three
hearts, and a lava-red kill floor waiting below. Hop between brick, ice,
water, and grass platforms, dodge icicles and patrolling critters, and grab
gems and money bags to build your score multiplier. Press any key or tap
to start. Full controls and stage notes:
[Lisa Climber How to Play](../lisa-climber/how-to-play.md).

### Lisetris

A falling-block puzzle with a love story. Move with `←`/`→` (or `A`/`D`),
soft drop with `↓`, hard drop with `Space`, rotate with `↑` or `X` (and back
with `Z`), and hold a piece with `C` or `Shift`. Classic mode speeds up as
you clear rows until the well tops out; Relaxed mode is exactly what it
sounds like. Full details:
[Lisetris How to Play](../lisetris/how-to-play.md).

### Lisa's Tapistry

A cozy tap-away mosaic puzzle. Each level is a grid of arrow tiles over a
hidden picture; tap a tile when the path ahead of its arrow is clear and it
slides off, revealing the beads underneath. Clear them all, in the right
order, to finish the mosaic. It plays with a tap (or arrow keys and `Enter`),
teaches itself in a short tutorial, and offers three boosters when you are
stuck. Full details:
[Lisa's Tapistry How to Play](../lisas-tapistry/how-to-play.md).

### Skyroute

SkyRoute Infinite is open-world flight. Pick the forgiving SL-9 Skylark or
the fast, slippery SwiftJet 100, choose a region and weather, and fly
airport to airport. The short version is simple. Brakes off with `B`, full throttle
with `W`, rotate at about 65 knots, and land gently for the score. There's
even an autopilot on `P` for when you'd rather enjoy the view. Full
checklists: [Skyroute How to Play](../skyroute/how-to-play.md).

### Spindrift

Pure vector arcade energy. Rotate with `←`/`→`, thrust with `↑`, fire with
`Space`, and save `Shift` hyperspace for genuine emergencies. Rocks are
worth 20, 50, or 100 points by size, saucers 200 or 1,000, and a bonus ship
arrives at 10,000. Your best run persists as the "HI" score. Chase it.
Full details: [Spindrift How to Play](../spindrift/how-to-play.md).
