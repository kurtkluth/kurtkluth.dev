Quick answers to the questions players actually ask. If yours is not here,
the [Overview](./overview.md) and [How to Play](./how-to-play.md) pages
cover the longer stories.

## Is it free?

Yes. Lisa Climber is free to play, with no account, no sign-up, and no
catch. It is the same deal as every Kluth Studios game.

## Do I need to install anything?

No. The game runs entirely in your browser at
[lisaclimber.kluthstudios.com](https://lisaclimber.kluthstudios.com).
Any current Chrome, Edge, Firefox, or Safari will do.

## Does it work on my phone?

Yes. The game supports touch as well as keyboard; the attract screen
literally says "press any key / tap to play." Climb at a desk or on the
couch, whichever suits.

## Is my high score saved?

Yes. Scores and progress live in your browser's local storage. That
means they come back whenever you return on the same device and browser,
but they will not follow you to a different device, and clearing your
browser's site data clears them too. There is no account or cloud sync.

## Why is it called Summit Smash and not Lisa Climber?

Both names are correct. **Lisa Climber** is the project's name in the
Kluth Studios collection, the name you will see on this site and on the
[Lisa Climber project page](/projects/lisa-climber). **Summit Smash** is
the title the game presents itself with when you play. One game, two hats.

## Who is Lisa?

The games are made for Lisa. Every project under the Kluth Studios banner
is built with her in mind, and this one carries her name right on the
label. (Her puzzle game, [Lisetris](../lisetris/overview.md), takes the
dedication even further.)

## How big is the climb?

Six stages, each built from eight layers of platforms, starting with the
icy Frostpeak. The HUD tracks both counts as you go, so you always know
how much mountain is left.
